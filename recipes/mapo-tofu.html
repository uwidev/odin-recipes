Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu is a dish of the Sichuan provence of China, or something like that. It's salty, umami, slightly sweet, spicy, and numbing. Pairs perfectly over white rice to help wash it down.

I think everyone should try the Mala flavor profile at least once in their lifetime.

Ingredients

  • 500g soft tofu (not silken)
  • 80g minced beef
  • 5 tbsp peanut oil or virgin rapeseed oil or indian mustard sed oil
  • 1 tbsp sichuan pepercorn
  • 3 tbsp sichuan chili bean paste aka pixian doubanjiang (郫县豆瓣酱)
  • 2 tbsp chili flakes
  • 1 tbsp black fermeneted beans aka douchi
  • 4 garlic cloves, minded
  • 2 inches ginger, minced
  • 1 cup of stock (usually chicken)
  • 1-2 stalks of green onion, whites and greens separated and chopped
  • seasoning: 1 1bsp light soy sauce; 1tbsp shaoxing wine; 1/4 tbsp msg; 1/4 tsp white pepper powder; 1/2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • cornstarch slurry: 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed in 1-2 tbsp water

Steps

  1. Cube tofu, into salted simmering water. Simmer ~3 minutes, reserve.
  2. Toast sichuan pepper corns until slightly darken and/or aromatic. Grind to powder, reserve.
  3. Chop garlic, ginger, green garlic, douchi, and chili bean paste. Reserve.
  4. Preheat pan/wok, high heat. Lightly oil for nonstick surface.
  5. Cook beef until it releases the oil it will absorb. It should be frying almost.
  6. Heat to medium. Throw in chili bean paste until oil is stained, about 90 seconds.
  7. Throw douchi, mix, then garlic, white green onion, ginger, mix. Chili flakes, mix.
  8. Fry for about a minute until an even paste.
  9. Add stock, strain tofu, add tofu, simmer ~7 minutes while occasionally gently stirring and until reduced about 1/3.
  10. Add seasoning, then half of cornstarch slurry. If thicker desired, more slurry.
  11. Top with green parts of green onion, mix.
  12. Spinkle Sichuan pepercorn. Heat off and serve.
